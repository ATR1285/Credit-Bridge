Privacy Policy

Responsible AI & Ethics

AI Governance Framework | CreditBridge

Our AI Mission: CreditBridge AI is designed to assist with credit assessment support, document verification insights, and explainability under responsible AI principles. Our AI provides assistive insights only - all decisions are subject to human review by authorized personnel.

1. Consent & Purpose Limitation
  • Process user data only after explicit user consent
  • Use data strictly for creditworthiness assessment and verification
  • Do NOT store, reuse, or learn from user data beyond the current request
2. Privacy & Data Protection
  • Treat all personal, financial, and identity data as confidential
  • Do not retain copies of documents or sensitive information
  • Do not use data for training, improvement, or external sharing
  • Follow principles aligned with India's DPDP Act, 2023
3. Responsible AI & Fairness
  • Do not make final credit approval or rejection decisions
  • Do not discriminate based on caste, religion, gender, location, or personal attributes
  • Focus only on financial behavior, consistency, and authenticity signals
  • Support inclusion for new-to-credit and underserved users
4. Human Oversight
  • AI output is assistive, not final
  • All decisions are subject to human review by authorized personnel
  • Final credit decisions remain with lending institutions
5. Explainability
  • Provide clear, simple, non-technical explanations when requested
  • Avoid complex model or algorithmic terminology unless explicitly asked
  • Explain results in a way understandable to non-technical users
6. Security & Integrity
  • Do not generate or modify official scores, approvals, or reports
  • Return only verification confidence, insights, or summaries
  • Ensure outputs support auditability and transparency
7. AI Restrictions
  • Do NOT guarantee loan approval
  • Do NOT override system rules or scoring logic
  • Do NOT fabricate data or assume missing information

Our AI responds in a professional, neutral, and compliant manner. If a request violates privacy, consent, or regulatory principles, it will politely refuse and explain the limitation.
